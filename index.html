Between Abstraction & Figuration

Born in Transylvania in 1953, Adrian Buba has emerged as one of the most compelling voices in contemporary art. His journey from the National University of Art in Bucharest to the galleries of Paris represents a unique synthesis of Eastern European intensity and Western artistic refinement.

René Huyghe praised Buba for "reconciling a certain figurative appearance with the free benefits of abstract art," a testament to his ability to navigate the liminal spaces between representation and pure form.

International Recognition

Adrian Buba's work is featured in prestigious public collections including the National Contemporary Art Fund (CNAP) in France, the National Art Museum of Romania, and the Ningbo Museum of Art in China, alongside numerous private collections worldwide.
